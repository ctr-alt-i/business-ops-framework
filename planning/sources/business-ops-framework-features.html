Business Operations Framework — Features & Functionality
Artifact-First Revision

This revision shifts the framework toward its most practical near-term purpose: handling the administrative and operational work around client engagements. The primary knowledge base should be formed by the actual working artifacts of the business — statements of work, contract amendments, requirements documents, retainers, time trackers, invoices, billing records, recorded calls, email threads, meeting materials, and related client assets — with structured knowledge extracted from and connected to those artifacts over time.

Admin-first Artifact-driven knowledge Recurring workflows Daily recap / todo loop Future work capture Meeting-to-admin automation

1. Product thesis

The first and most valuable version of this framework should function as an agentic administrative operating system for client work: capturing operational artifacts, keeping future work visible, and automating end-of-day and post-meeting administrative processes.

Rather than starting as a broad abstract knowledge graph or generic project system, the platform should begin from the actual materials used to run the business. Knowledge should emerge from those materials and remain linked back to them.

Among the most important primary artifacts are recorded calls and email threads, because those are often the main places where business users communicate feedback, confirm approvals, clarify scope, make decisions, and request changes.

In other words, the system should answer questions like:

  • What did we agree to deliver?
  • What changed commercially or operationally?
  • What work happened today and what still needs to happen?
  • What was said in recent calls that implies future work, risk, or expansion?
  • What admin work should run automatically after the day’s work is done?

2. Primary jobs to be done

Handle administrative work

Manage the operational artifacts and recurring admin tasks that surround real client delivery.

Keep future work visible

Capture possible follow-on work, expansions, changes, and discovery signals from calls, email, and messages.

Run the daily operating loop

Provide a beginning-of-day recap/todo view and a late-day or post-call admin execution cycle.

Generate clean downstream outputs

Turn meetings and work activity into recaps, notes, wiki updates, action items, time logs, and commercial artifacts.

Capture approvals & decisions

Treat recorded calls and email threads as first-class business artifacts where approvals, feedback, changes, and decisions are actually made.

Core user-facing outcomes

  • A consultant or operator starts the day with a clear, current recap and todo list.
  • After meetings and hands-on work, the system automatically performs the admin clean-up work.
  • Commercial and delivery artifacts stay current and queryable.
  • Future opportunities and follow-on work are captured continuously, not lost in conversations.
  • Decisions, action items, approvals, and operational changes get reflected in the knowledge/wiki layer with minimal manual effort.

3. Artifact-first knowledge base

The knowledge base should be primarily composed of the artifacts and materials actually used to operate the business, rather than only abstract notes. Structured knowledge should be extracted from those artifacts and linked across clients, projects, decisions, and workflows.

Critically, recorded calls and email threads should be treated as first-class source artifacts, not just optional inputs, because they are often the primary channels where feedback is given, approvals are confirmed, scope is negotiated, and business decisions are made.

3.1 Primary artifact classes

Artifact class Purpose in the system Examples
Commercial agreements Define scope, terms, rates, obligations, and change history Statements of Work, contract amendments, retainers
Pre-sales artifacts Capture how opportunities are framed, sold, and proposed before formal execution begins Pre-sales decks, proposals, opportunity briefs, discovery summaries
Requirements artifacts Capture what is being built, validated, or delivered Functional requirements docs, technical requirements docs, implementation notes
Time & billing records Track work performed and support invoicing / commercial follow-through Time trackers, invoice drafts, invoice records, billing support logs
Decision & approval communications Capture approvals, feedback, commercial direction, scope changes, and business decisions at their source Recorded calls, call transcripts, email threads, approval chains, client feedback emails
Operational conversation records Capture commitments, open questions, working context, and future work signals Meeting notes, chat threads, message exports, internal coordination notes
Delivery artifacts Store outputs used to manage and communicate project work Status updates, recaps, decks, wiki pages, decision memos
Testing / evidence artifacts Preserve proof, validation, or reference material linked to work performed Test results, screenshots, validation notes, QA summaries

3.2 Knowledge extracted from artifacts

  • scope commitments and constraints
  • deliverables and milestones
  • stakeholders and roles
  • decisions and decision rationale
  • approvals, signoffs, and unresolved approval requests
  • feedback, objections, and change signals
  • action items and owners
  • time/billing implications
  • future work opportunities or change requests
  • operational lessons learned

3.3 Knowledge base behavior

  • Each artifact should remain a durable source object.
  • Extracted structured knowledge should stay linked to its source artifact.
  • The system should support both document-style retrieval and structured query.
  • Knowledge wiki pages should be maintained as cleaned, consolidated views of source activity.
  • Decisions, action items, and changes should be continuously reconciled back into the knowledge layer.
Design principle: do not treat the wiki as the source of truth. Treat it as a cleaned and organized derivative view of the artifact and activity layer.

4. Future work tracking

Future work tracking should be a first-class subsystem. It should continuously capture possible next-phase work, new requirements, changes in scope, upsell/expansion signals, and longer-term opportunities emerging from discovery and ongoing client conversation.

4.1 Sources of future work signals

  • recorded calls and transcripts
  • approval and feedback emails
  • email threads
  • chat or message conversations
  • meeting recaps and action items
  • requirements discussions and change requests
  • delivery friction, recurring issues, or repeated asks

4.2 Future work functionality

Capture

Automatically detect and log future work candidates from conversations and artifacts.

Qualification

Classify signals as idea, request, risk, change, expansion, or follow-on project.

Linkage

Attach future work items to client, project, artifact, meeting, and commercial context.

Promotion

Turn future work into tasks, backlog items, proposal inputs, amendments, or renewal materials.

4.3 Output examples

  • future opportunity backlog
  • potential amendment candidates
  • proposal follow-up notes
  • client roadmap suggestions
  • renewal / expansion input briefs

5. Workday workflow

The framework should support a clear operating rhythm for the workday. This is not just project tracking — it is daily operational coordination.

5.1 Beginning-of-day workflow

The start of the day should produce a todo / recap briefing.

  • today’s meetings and relevant prep context
  • open action items by client and priority
  • work that should be completed today
  • missing follow-ups from prior days
  • time/billing reminders
  • admin tasks that are due or overdue
  • future work signals that need qualification
  • meetings that need to be scheduled, confirmed, or followed up on

5.2 During-the-day workflow

  • capture calls, transcripts, key email threads, approvals, and messages
  • associate them with clients, projects, and existing artifacts
  • surface likely action items, decisions, and approval events in near real time
  • identify when a follow-up meeting or scheduling action is needed
  • track hands-on-keyboard work that may need to be logged or turned into deliverable artifacts

5.3 Post-call / post-communication workflow

After each call, recorded discussion, or important email thread, the framework should be able to generate:

  • meeting notes / recaps
  • action items and owners
  • decision summaries
  • approval status or approval requests
  • future work signals
  • meeting scheduling suggestions or follow-up scheduling drafts
  • wiki or knowledge updates
  • time-entry suggestions

5.4 Late-day / closeout workflow

After the day’s calls and hands-on work are done, scheduled workflows should execute the administrative closeout layer. 

Start of day
  -> recap + todos + meeting prep
During day
  -> collect work signals + conversations + artifacts
After each call / key communication
  -> recap + actions + decisions + approvals + future work extraction + scheduling follow-up
End of day / scheduled closeout
  -> time logging
  -> artifact generation
  -> wiki cleanup
  -> decisions cleanup
  -> approval reconciliation
  -> action item reconciliation
  -> billing/admin updates

6. Scheduled admin workflows

Scheduled workflows should handle the repetitive admin layer so users do not have to manually reconstruct and close the loop each day.

6.1 Core scheduled workflows

Daily admin closeout End of business day time log suggestions, wiki cleanup, action reconciliation, decision capture, approval cleanup

6.2 Scheduled workflow requirements

  • time-based scheduling
  • event-based triggering
  • rerun/reconcile capability
  • human approval for selected outputs
  • audit trail of what ran and what changed

8. Asset & admin infrastructure

The framework should provide lightweight but durable infrastructure for the actual files and records used in administrative operations.

8.1 Asset handling requirements

Storage awareness

Track where artifacts live across drive systems, local storage, or external platforms.

Metadata

Associate client, project, stage, owner, date, artifact class, and source conversation.

Linkage

Connect files to meetings, decisions, tasks, invoices, time entries, and future work items.

Lineage

Show which source records led to which generated outputs or cleaned wiki entries.

Versioning awareness

Differentiate canonical documents, drafts, amendments, and derivative summaries.

Experimentation support

Allow testing model interactions with operational artifacts without destabilizing source records.

8.2 Typical admin artifacts to support first

  • SOWs and amendments
  • pre-sales decks and proposals
  • functional and technical requirements docs
  • retainer and scope records
  • time trackers and time-entry suggestions
  • invoice records and billing support notes
  • recorded calls, transcripts, and meeting recordings
  • email threads, approval chains, and client feedback emails
  • meeting notes, recaps, and follow-ups
  • decision logs and action item trackers

9. Project management / admin agent capabilities

The core agent should operate less like a generic assistant and more like an operations coordinator + admin orchestrator.

9.1 Core capabilities

  • assemble current-day operational context
  • generate beginning-of-day recap and todo packet
  • process new calls, emails, approval threads, and messages into structured outputs
  • identify decisions, approvals, actions, and future work
  • recommend and draft time logs
  • generate or update admin artifacts
  • facilitate next-meeting scheduling and follow-up meeting coordination
  • maintain clean client wiki / knowledge views
  • support billing and invoicing workflows with linked evidence

9.2 Agent outputs

  • daily recap / todo list
  • post-call meeting notes and recap email drafts
  • meeting scheduling suggestions, next-meeting prompts, or scheduling drafts
  • approval status summaries and unresolved approval queues
  • action item tracker updates
  • decision log updates
  • time entry suggestions or completed time logs
  • new artifact drafts
  • wiki cleanup summaries
  • future work candidate logs

11. Communication-first suggestions

Because business users make a large share of their decisions in calls and email, the framework should treat those channels as primary operational systems — not just as places to fetch text from.

11.1 New design suggestion: decision / approval inbox

Add a dedicated layer that tracks decision and approval events extracted from recorded calls and email threads.

  • pending approvals
  • confirmed approvals
  • rejected or revised items
  • ambiguous decisions requiring clarification
  • approvals linked to artifacts like SOWs, requirements docs, invoices, or follow-up actions

11.2 New design suggestion: source communications should be canonical evidence

Calls and emails should not just feed summaries; they should remain queryable evidence for why a decision, approval, or scope change was recorded.

  • Every important approval or decision should point back to the call, transcript section, or email thread where it happened.
  • Derived wiki pages and clean summaries should preserve source references.
  • Commercial and operational changes should be auditable against source communications.

11.3 New design suggestion: communication-to-artifact reconciliation

The system should regularly reconcile new communication activity against existing artifacts.

  • If an email changes scope, flag the affected SOW or requirements doc.
  • If a call confirms an approval, update the approval state for the relevant artifact.
  • If a message implies billable work, suggest time and billing updates.
  • If feedback indicates future work, create or update a future work candidate.

11.4 New design suggestion: separate raw communications from cleaned knowledge

Architecturally, it will help to distinguish three layers: 

Layer 1: Source communications
- recorded calls
- transcripts
- email threads
- messages

Layer 2: Canonical business artifacts
- pre-sales decks
- proposals
- SOWs
- amendments
- requirements docs
- time trackers
- invoices
- action trackers

Layer 3: Derived knowledge views
- wiki pages
- decision logs
- approval summaries
- future work backlogs
- daily recap packets

This separation will make the system easier to reason about, audit, and extend later.

11.5 New design suggestion: support a commercial artifact progression

Since future work often becomes pre-sales material and then formal delivery scope, the system should explicitly support a progression like: 

Future work signal
  -> qualified opportunity
  -> proposal input
  -> pre-sales deck / proposal
  -> approved scope
  -> SOW / amendment
  -> delivery artifact set

This would help bridge the gap between conversational signals, sales material, commercial agreements, and operational execution.

12. MVP feature set

The MVP should focus on making the operator’s admin layer dramatically easier, not on solving every aspect of business operations at once.

12.1 Suggested MVP features

  • Artifact repository + metadata layer for SOWs, amendments, pre-sales decks, proposals, requirements docs, time trackers, invoices, recorded calls, transcripts, and email threads
  • Morning recap / todo workflow generated from meetings, open actions, overdue admin work, pending approvals, future work signals, and scheduling needs
  • Post-call / post-email processing workflow for meeting notes, decisions, approvals, action items, follow-up draft creation, and scheduling facilitation
  • Daily admin closeout workflow for time logging, wiki cleanup, action reconciliation, approval cleanup, and artifact generation
  • Future work tracking module fed by calls, emails, and messages
  • Decision / approval reconciliation workflow that updates source artifacts and clean knowledge views
  • Knowledge/wiki update workflow that turns raw activity into cleaned operational knowledge
  • Billing support workflow that links time and operational activity to invoice prep

12.2 MVP success criteria

  • The user starts every day with a useful operational recap.
  • Meeting scheduling and follow-up coordination are surfaced at the right moments with minimal manual effort.
  • Most post-meeting admin work is automated or heavily assisted.
  • Time logging becomes easier and more evidence-backed.
  • Important decisions, approvals, and action items stop getting lost.
  • Future work signals are captured consistently.
  • Operational artifacts become queryable and reusable rather than buried in folders and threads.

13. Extensibility direction

Even though the initial system is focused on administrative operations, it should still be designed as a framework with stable extension points.

Artifact adapters

Custom support for new document classes, billing formats, contract types, and asset repositories.

Workflow plugins

Client-specific or team-specific admin workflows, closeout flows, and reporting cadences.

Knowledge extractors

Custom extraction logic for decisions, actions, commercial terms, and future work signals.

Integration connectors

Google, Microsoft, CRM, billing, or transcription integrations as reusable modules.

Agent roles

Separate agents for recap generation, wiki maintenance, billing support, and future work review.

Policy modules

Approval rules, retention rules, billing controls, and visibility permissions.
